# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Anyone interested in film culture in Dresden — campus affiliation is incidental, not required. The copy is explicit about this ("Du musst nicht an der TU studieren"): a TU Dresden student and a Dresden local who's never set foot on campus are addressed the same way. Their context is deciding, on a given week, whether to spend an evening at a screening — often on short notice, checking what's on and whether it's worth the trip to the Hörsaalzentrum or the courtyard.

## Product Purpose

Kino im Kasten (KiK) is a student-run cinema at TU Dresden. The site exists to get people into seats: it publishes the program, the Sommerkino open-air series, and one-off events (guest talks, themed series, 35mm nights), and gives newcomers enough context (what it is, where, that it's open to everyone) to show up. A secondary membership area (login/profile) supports the small group who help run screenings. Success is measured in attendance — people who see the site and actually come to a film.

## Positioning

Free, student-curated, and open to everyone — a film culture alternative to Dresden's commercial cinemas, run by people who care about the program rather than the box office.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: "Programm ansehen" (see the program / go to a screening). Secondary CTA: "Mitmachen" (join the team) — recruitment matters but rides behind attendance.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "Filmkultur in Dresden. Kuratiert von Studierenden."
- Belief ladder: (1) this is a real, happening-soon event with a concrete time and place — (2) it's free or cheap and I don't need to be a TU student to go — (3) it's curated with care, so it's worth the evening.
- Proof on hand: none yet. The current homepage logo wall (TU Dresden, Letterboxd, Mubi, Vimeo, Spotify) is placeholder content, not real partners or press — replace with actual sponsors, partners, or coverage once available; until then, prefer specific, concrete program details (dates, venues, curators' voice) over a placeholder trust bar.

## Brand Personality

Warm and welcoming first, cinephile and curatorial close behind — knowledgeable and taste-driven about film without turning academic or gatekeeping. The tone should read like an invitation from people who love film and want you there, not a program note from a film studies department.

## Anti-references

Explicitly not: commercial multiplex chains (CineStar/Cinemaxx-style corporate cinema marketing), generic startup/SaaS landing-page grammar (gradient hero, feature grids, trust-bar clichés), or a sterile TU Dresden bureaucratic administration page. KiK is student-run and should never read as a business or an institution.

## Design Principles

- Show, don't sell — a concrete next screening (film, date, venue, price) does more work than any marketing copy.
- No gate, no jargon — every page should make it obvious that no TU affiliation, film-studies vocabulary, or insider knowledge is required to show up.
- Curated, not corporate — warmth and care over polish-for-its-own-sake; DIY charm is an asset, not a flaw to sand off.
- Program is the hero — the homepage and navigation should always foreground what's actually playing, not the institution behind it.
- Recruitment rides behind attendance — the membership/team CTA supports the site but never competes with "come to a screening" for primary attention.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA: solid color contrast, full keyboard navigation, screen-reader support for program listings and event details, and a working `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for any motion design. No unusual constraints beyond that.
